# 🏆 Hell UI/UX - Hackathon Winner

This project secured **1st Place** in a high-intensity hackathon! Built by **Team KAAM CHALAU CODER** (Aditya Singh & Purjeet Shahu).

## 📝 Problem Statement
The challenge was to take existing frontend patterns and build something highly creative, unique, and intentionally confusing. We built a frustrating, chaotic, "dark-pattern" food delivery website that playfully flips traditional UX rules on their head.

## 🚀 The Journey
- **Coding Round:** We started strong by securing **1st place** in the initial coding elimination round.
- **Ideation & Prototyping:** Once the final problem statement was revealed, we spent the first 30 minutes rapidly sketching pen-and-paper prototypes to design the most confusing yet brilliant interactions.
- **Execution:** The entire application code was written and brought to life in just **1 hour**!

## 🤯 The Features (Our Beautiful UX Disasters)
1. **The $1 Entry Toll:** The landing page forces users to pay $1 to enter. Trying to click "Exit" causes the button to shrink and dodge your cursor while playing sound effects. You *must* pay to proceed.
2. **The Judgmental Menu:** We broke the "Add to Cart" buttons intentionally. Users must click the food image itself instead, at which point the app ruthlessly judges their food choices (e.g., ordering pizza triggers a "Mote Healthy kha!" insult).
3. **The Rickroll Cart:** Trying to view your cart immediately blasts a Rickroll meme video.
4. **The Gatekept Checkout:** Before you can pay, you must pass a timed General Knowledge quiz. If you answer incorrectly, your cart gets wiped, and you are sent back to the beginning with an insulting "You Dumbass!" alert.
5. **The Memory-Loss Form:** If you manage to reach the payment page, the site conveniently "forgets" all your details, forcing you to fill out login/signup info all over again. Oh, and we only accept UPI for payment!
6. **3D Chaos:** Every page includes chaotic 3D background components built with attractive colors and jarring transitions.
